Stress Analysis Help

Understanding Stress

Stress is the internal force per unit area within a material. It's calculated as σ = F/A, where F is the applied force and A is the cross-sectional area.

Safety Factors

Safety factors account for uncertainties in loading, material properties, and design. A factor of 2-4 is typical for static loading conditions.

Material Selection

Choose materials based on strength requirements, environmental conditions, and cost considerations. Steel offers high strength, while aluminum provides good strength-to-weight ratio.
